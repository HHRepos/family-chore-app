Iterating on the Garden

Variations & the Day-32 future state.

Three things from your last note: the wandering creatures shouldn't flip when they change direction, you wanted to see what a 30-day garden actually looks like, and you wanted alternative aesthetic directions for the Garden — different palettes, different characters. All three answered below.

1 — The wander fix

The previous animation used scaleX(-1) to flip the cat when it changed direction. Looked janky. Fixed: creatures now drift one way across the screen and fade out at the edge, with a fade-in at the start for the next cycle. Same pattern reused for the deer in the glade.

/* Old — flipped on direction change (looked weird) */
@keyframes wander {
  0%, 100% { transform: translateX(0); }
  40%      { transform: translateX(60px) scaleX(-1); /* ← flips */ }
}

/* New — drifts across, fades in/out, no flip */
@keyframes drift {
  0%   { transform: translateX(-40px); opacity: 0; }
  15%  { opacity: 1; }
  85%  { opacity: 1; }
  100% { transform: translateX(80px); opacity: 0; }
}

The Living Garden page has been updated. Reload and the cat should now look like a pet passing through, not pacing a cage.

What's grown after 30 days

The tree

Comparing day 1 to day 32: the tree has visibly more layers of foliage, a thicker trunk, and a small wood-knot detail. Growth is gradual — the tree gets a tiny new layer roughly every 10 days, so day 7 looks slightly bigger than day 1, day 30 looks substantially bigger, day 90 has cherry-blossom petals or autumn colour depending on time of year.

Past flowers cluster as beds

Every chore the kid completes leaves a small flower behind permanently. After 30 days × 3-5 chores/day, that's 100+ flowers. They cluster into "beds" by category — toothbrush flowers form one bed, pet-care flowers another. Today's three flowers are rendered large and bright; the historical ones are smaller and dimmer in the background. Density without noise.

Wildlife population

Wildlife joins the garden as the kid spends seeds in the glade. Day 12 they bought a hedgehog. Day 25 they saved up enough for the deer. The deer wanders across the meadow in the upper-middle. Three fireflies float up from the ground near the tree. A butterfly crosses near the right-hand flower bed.

A tiny bench, a sign, a knot

The garden accumulates small objects of meaning: a wooden bench appears around day 14 (a bonus from a 7-day streak). The "DAY 32" sign is a permanent post that updates with the day count. Tiny details that the kid earns and notices but never has to manage.

The card stack stays the same

Today's chores are still three cards, just like day 1. The kid's daily ritual doesn't get more complex over time — only the background of the garden grows. Today's task list stays calm regardless of how many days they've put in.

Day 90 and beyond

Beyond 90 days the tree may shed leaves to reflect autumn (real- world season-based), then bud blossoms in spring. Wildlife can nest. The kid's avatar can sit on the bench in idle states. The garden becomes a living world that's been steadily tended.

4 — Character & creature system

Right now the mockups use stock emoji (🐱 🦋 🦔). They work for a v1, but they all look like they belong to different visual languages — iOS-default emoji are inconsistent in style, scale, and personality. For a polished launch, replace key creatures with custom illustrated characters that feel like they all live in the same world.

Below, four character archetypes for the Living Garden. These are quick SVG silhouettes — actual production art would be commissioned illustrations or Lottie animations, but the cast is what matters.

The Sapling
Garden spirit · welcomes the kid · first-run guide
The Companion
Real-world pet (cat / dog / hamster, parent-customisable)
The Wildling
Bought from glade · pure decoration · no chores
The Gardener
The kid's avatar · walks in the garden · customisable

Why custom characters matter

Emoji approach (current)Custom characters (proposed)
Inconsistent style — each emoji renders differently across iOS versions One designer's hand. Same line weight, same proportions, same world.
No personality — 🐱 is just a cat icon The Companion has a name, a sleeping pose, a curious pose, a happy pose
Can't animate beyond CSS transforms Lottie animations: blinks, tail wags, head turns, stretches
Can't tell a story Sapling appears at first-run, hedgehog joins on day 12, deer on day 25 — a cast that grows
Can't show emotion specific to the moment Sapling looks proud after a streak, sad after a missed day. Cat acknowledges the kid's return.

Concrete: I'd commission ~12 characters total (Sapling, 4 pet variants for parents to pick, 6 wildlings, the avatar) and pair each with 3-4 Lottie animations. ~4 weeks of illustration work. Could be a phased thing — ship v1 with emoji, swap them in over 2-3 sprints.
