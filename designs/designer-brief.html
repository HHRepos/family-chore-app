Designer Brief · OMyDays · v1.0

The Living Garden.

A children's family-ritual app that needs an illustrator to bring a living, painted world to life. The selected aesthetic is watercolour softness with Ghibli-grade magic — calm and pastel as the foundation, with fireflies, dappled light, and a small cast of recurring characters layered on top.

This brief is what you'd hand to the illustrator. It's structured for a freelance / studio engagement: aesthetic direction, full asset list, animation specification, deliverable formats, phasing, and review cadence. ~30 static illustrations + ~15 Lottie animations across a 4–6 week engagement.

Project at a glance

ProductOMyDays — a family ritual app where kids tend a small living garden by completing daily routines
AudiencePrimary: kids age 5–12. Secondary: parents.
PlatformiOS (SwiftUI). Currently in TestFlight beta.
ToneCalm, magical, hand-painted. Anti-gamified. The opposite of neon.
ConstraintThe garden is alive even when nothing's happening. Idle states matter as much as interaction states.
Star momentThe 600ms swipe-to-water gesture: card swipes up → 3 droplets arc to a flower → flower opens → ripple lingers 5s. Everything else is context for that moment.

Aesthetic direction

Take watercolour as the base — soft pastel palette, hand-painted edges, gentle shapes — and layer on the magic-realism of a Studio Ghibli forest: dappled sunlight through a canopy, occasional fireflies, a small mossy detail at the base of a tree, the sense that something might wander into frame at any moment.

Reference points (do not copy): the watercolour textures of Beatrix Potter, the calm interiors of Studio Ghibli's My Neighbour Totoro, the colour and pacing of Sky: Children of the Light. Not Animal Crossing (too cartoon), not Stardew Valley (too pixel), not Disney (too saccharine).

Visual language rules

  • Soft edges. No hard outlines. Stroke implied by colour transition, not pen weight.
  • Pastel base, warm highlights. Sky pastels at 30–40% saturation. Highlights and ambient particles at 70–80% saturation, used sparingly.
  • Dappled light. A subtle warm-amber gradient overlay on every screen, suggesting sunlight filtering through canopy.
  • Magic in the margins, not the foreground. Fireflies, dust motes, falling leaves, a garden spirit that occasionally walks past — never centre-stage, always at the periphery.
  • Painterly, not vector-perfect. Slight texture, hand-finished feel. Geometry is suggested, not enforced.
  • Time-of-day shifts. Dawn (warm pinks), day (sage greens), dusk (amber + violet), night (deep blue with stars). Same garden, four moods.

What we are not

  • Not gamified — no XP bars, no levels visible, no badge collections in the art.
  • Not cartoonish — characters have personality, but they're painted, not bouncy.
  • Not minimal/Scandinavian — soft and rich, not flat and geometric.
  • Not retro / pixel-art.

Deliverables

  • Static illustrations — SVG (vector). Each at 1x intended display size, exported with stroke widths normalized.
  • Lottie animations — JSON (Lottie 5.7+). One per file, named consistently. Frame rate: 60fps. Easing curves locked.
  • Style sheet — A one-page PDF showing the painted texture style, brush behaviour, line weight, shadow rules. Will be referenced by all future contributors.
  • Source files — Procreate / Illustrator / After Effects, whatever the illustrator works in. Owned by us at delivery.
  • Variants — Each character and tree at light + dark for time-of-day. Sky gradients at 4 times.

Process & phasing

Four phases over ~5 weeks. Each phase ends in a review checkpoint — designer presents, we approve, we move on.

PhaseDurationDeliverables
1 · Style sample 1 week The Sapling + 1 cat companion + 1 hedgehog wildling + the Day-1 tree, all in the chosen aesthetic. Locks the visual language. Paid even if we don't proceed.
2 · Character cast 2 weeks Remaining 5 companion species, 7 remaining wildlings, gardener avatar with all customisations.
3 · World & growth 1.5 weeks 6 tree growth stages, 4 sky gradients, 4 flower categories with bud/bloom states, ground textures, glade scene, pond.
4 · Animations & polish 1.5 weeks All Lottie animations, the swipe-to-water sequence (the demo moment), edge cases (empty garden first-run, missed-day brown leaf), style sheet PDF.

Budget guidance

For a senior illustrator with native Lottie / After Effects experience, expect £8,000–14,000 for the full pack at this scope (or ~$10–18k USD). Higher end for studios; lower end for an independent illustrator looking for a portfolio piece in app design. The Phase 1 sample alone usually runs £800–1,500.

Where to find the right person:

  • Behance / Dribbble — search "watercolour app illustration", "Ghibli illustration", "Lottie children's app"
  • Twitter / Bluesky — illustrators in the kids' app space (Headspace, Calm, Mindstrong all use independents)
  • Society6 / Etsy — illustrators with a watercolour Etsy store often take commission work
  • Lottie's own marketplace — illustrators who already work in Lottie format

Vetting — three things to look for

  • Have they shipped animated illustrations (Lottie / video / GIF) before? Static portfolio doesn't translate automatically to motion.
  • Can they make a face that's expressive without being cartoonish? Show them The Sapling reference and ask for a 30-min sketch — see what they do.
  • Do they understand iOS asset pipelines (1x/2x/3x, dark mode, Dynamic Type)? Saves us a week of reformatting.

What success looks like

A 7-year-old opens the app for the first time. The Sapling appears, smiles, addresses them by name. Their tree sprouts. The cat walks in. A firefly drifts up. The kid says "oh" and means it. They show their parent. The parent says "that's beautiful" and means it. That's the bar.

If the illustration achieves a calm magic that both the kid and the parent want to look at, we've succeeded. If it looks like a commodity children's app that uses a forest as wallpaper, we've failed.
